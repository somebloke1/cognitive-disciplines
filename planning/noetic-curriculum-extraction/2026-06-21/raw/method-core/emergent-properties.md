# Emergent Properties of Framework Development

**Source:** `.method_sources/emergent_probability.txt` (the author)
**Date:** 2026-02-28

This document articulates the twelve consequent properties of emergent probability and their correspondence to agent framework development. These properties are derived fromthe author's analysis of world process governed by emergent probability.

---

## The Twelve Consequent Properties

### 1. Succession of Situations

Each moment is characterized by:
- The schemes actually functioning
- The further schemes that have become concretely possible
- The current schedule of probabilities

**Framework correspondence:** Project state at any moment — what's working, what's now possible, what's likely to emerge or break down.

### 2. Open Process

World process is a succession of probable realizations of possibilities — neither deterministic nor random.

**Framework correspondence:** Framework development is open. It doesn't follow fixed rails, but it's also not arbitrary. Probable realizations emerge from conditions.

### 3. Increasingly Systematic

The further the series of schemes is realized, the greater the systematization.

**Framework correspondence:** As the framework develops, it becomes more structured. Later schemes (succession, decisions) depend on earlier schemes (imperatives, constitution).

### 4. Assurability

Sufficient numbers and time assure the realization of even low-probability schemes.

**Framework correspondence:** Persistence pays off. Attempts that seem unlikely to succeed can become assured through repetition and patience.

### 5. Initial Situation Limited

Only possibilities and probabilities in the initial state matter. The determinist's desire for complete initial information is misplaced.

**Framework correspondence:** Don't over-specify initial conditions. What matters is what can emerge, not exhaustive upfront planning.

### 6. Enormous Differentiation

The totality of possibilities can be realized according to probability schedules.

**Framework correspondence:** The framework can branch in many directions. Given sufficient operation, diverse possibilities actualize.

### 7. Breakdowns

Every scheme has a probability of breakdown. Earlier failures cascade to dependent schemes.

**Framework correspondence:** Tools fail, sessions crash, successions break. Plan for breakdown. Map dependencies to understand cascade paths.

### 8. Blind Alleys

High-survival schemes can trap materials in routines that block development.

**Framework correspondence:** Stable patterns can imprison. Periodically ask: is this serving development, or has it become a constraint?

### 9. Narrowing Distribution

Later schemes have narrower distribution than earlier — they can only occur where earlier schemes are already functioning.

**Framework correspondence:** Advanced features depend on foundations. The deeper in the conditioned series, the narrower the basis for emergence.

### 10. Time Compensation

Narrower basis requires longer time intervals for realization.

**Framework correspondence:** When the path is narrow, patience is required. Rushing doesn't accelerate emergence.

### 11. Numbers Compensation

Greater probability of blind alleys and breakdowns requires larger initial numbers to assure realization.

**Framework correspondence:** If the environment is hostile (many breakdowns, many traps), increase attempts. More starting points ensure at least one succeeds.

### 12. Generic and Invariant

These properties cannot be upset by any amount of particular scientific work. They are relatively invariant features of any process governed by emergent probability.

**Framework correspondence:** These properties hold regardless of specific implementation. They are not hypotheses subject to revision.

## Principle of Correspondence

> "Significantly different underlying manifolds require different higher integrations. Within limits, the same higher integration will systematize differing manifolds; the point to the principle is that these limits exist and that to transgress them is to eliminate the higher integration." — the author

The twelve properties are a higher integration for world process. The EP Audit extension's output schema is a higher integration for the agent framework manifold — informed by the twelve, but in correspondence with our operational reality.
