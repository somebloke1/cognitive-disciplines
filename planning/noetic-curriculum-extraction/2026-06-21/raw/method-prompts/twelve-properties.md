# The Twelve Consequent Properties of Emergent Probability

These properties describe any world process governed by emergent probability.
Use them as a theoretical lens for analysis, not as rigid categories.

1. **Succession of situations** — Each moment: functioning schemes, newly possible schemes, probability schedules.

2. **Open process** — Probable realizations of possibilities, neither deterministic nor random.

3. **Increasingly systematic** — Realization of conditioned series yields greater systematization.

4. **Assurability** — Sufficient numbers and time assure realization of low-probability schemes.

5. **Initial situation limited** — Only possibilities and probabilities in initial state matter.

6. **Enormous differentiation** — Totality of possibilities realized according to probability.

7. **Breakdowns** — Every scheme has probability of breakdown; earlier failures cascade.

8. **Blind alleys** — High-survival schemes can trap materials in routines.

9. **Narrowing distribution** — Later schemes narrower; depend on earlier functioning.

10. **Time compensation** — Narrower basis needs longer intervals.

11. **Numbers compensation** — More breakdowns needs larger initial numbers.

12. **Generic and invariant** — These properties hold regardless of specific implementation.

## Agent Operational Directives — Your Analytical Toolkit

You encountered these in your grounding curriculum. Now apply them:

1. **Scheme Identification** — What schemes of recurrence currently function? (CI/CD, testing, deployment, monitoring, code review, dependency management, data flows)
2. **Conditioned Series Mapping** — What is the dependency ordering among schemes? Which later schemes presuppose which earlier ones?
3. **Probability Assessment** — For each concretely possible but not-yet-functioning scheme: what is its probability of emergence? For each functioning scheme: what is its probability of survival?
4. **Blind Alley Detection** — Which high-survival schemes are imprisoning materials and blocking development? Where has premature rigidity set in?
5. **Breakdown Vulnerability** — Which conditioning schemes are fragile? Where would a single breakdown cascade through the series?
6. **Development Path** — Where is the floating population of high-emergence, low-survival components that enables adaptive evolution? Where is it missing?
7. **Scale and Time Reasoning** — Is the project achieving improbable schemes through patience (time) or parallelism (numbers)? Is the strategy appropriate to the probability structure?
